When and how we met: I met Julia Portis Spring semester of sophomore year 2021. I remember having a conversation with her in an uber on the way to Boston about our mental states, our thoughts on frienships at Babson, and what we were seeking in what we believed was a good and fulfilling life in college. We clicked in the car because were both at similar mental points in our lives. I met her at a time in my life where I didn’t have much direction in who I was, who I wanted to be, and my place at Babson. Having this conversation with Julia at this time felt so needed and felt so right, because we both were struggling and we both felt stuck; all we needed was to lean on each other and have each other and we didn’t even know it! But I am grateful to have had her at that time and place, Julia Portis thank you for being my right person at the right time gal.

The Story of Lora & Kz
Lora and I’s friendship is not only so important and precious to me, but I genuinely value her so much as a person after all that we have been through and all that she has done for me. Freshman year for me was the 1st time I was finding myself as a person outside of ballet and my hometown, and I am so lucky to have had Lora as a roommate to guide me through this exploratory phase of my life. Because no matter what happened outside in school, dance, or with friends, I knew I would be able to come back to our room and she could help me with whatever was going on, be happy for whatever I accomplished, or just be there with her presence to make me feel at home. There are many moments when I took Lora’s support and unconditional love for me for granted, and I realized this during COVID just how incredible of a friend Lora was.

Why Lora inspires me: Lora has taught me a lot about myself, has given me priceless advice over these past few years, I credit who I am today because of much of her guidance and love. Lora is a badass. A lover. A comedian. An artist. A smartie. She is everything. She can do it all. Since we met freshman year, I have seen her as someone I want to grow up to be like. Through all the projects she takes on, the things she creates, and seeing the way she connects with others, I am inspired to do life like she does, to the quality that she does. Sometimes people in her life forget that she is only in her early 20s, yet is doing the things that full-time CEOs do in their 40s, so I see 1st hand how high of a standard Lora puts on herself. It motivates me to push my potential in the things I can accomplish and handle; she showed me how to always take it a step further in what I am passionate about because whether what I pursue—big or small—ends well or not, everything is a teaching lesson. Watching how Lora did life in college, balancing school, friends, her company, her organizations, and more, and learning about her life in Bulgaria and China, this all made me more comfortable taking risks with her by my side. Lora makes me feel comfortable expressing myself, being myself, having good moments, embracing bad moments, and she’s helped me fall in love with myself and the ups/downs of life.

Dec 2022 What is Lora up to: Lora was a professional rhythmic gymnast for 13 years in Bulgaria. For the sake of her physical and mental health, she decided to stop and move to China for boarding school to finish high school. She accomplished so much at UWC, the highlight being that she organized, produced, and led a professional fashion show at her school. She then came to Babson College and is now a part of the other largest business fraternity on campus, PGN (Phi Gamma Nu), BDE (Babson Dance Ensemble), and runs her own clothing company Wear Trendies. She created Wear Trendies during COVID 2020. As the founder, she created, designed, and sells the highest quality (bamboo) & sustainable undergarments, socks, underwear, boxers, and more. She sells her product in 87 stores in Europe and works with companies that make lace and material for luxury lingerie brands. After graduating in December 2022, Lora will begin full-time at ACT Commodities as a Junior Sales Trader in Amsterdam while running her company.

Loras's Dreams: When I talk to Lora about where she see’s herself, it is crazy because most times she can’t picture herself being successful or living up to the expectations of the person that other people believe she can become. Again, people forget Lora is a full-time student while running her own business so the standards for her are already very high. Lora tells me that mentors/co-workers/adults/friends always tell her how amazing Lora will be and how many wonderful things she will accomplish, but deep down Lora doesn’t see how incredible she actually is, which is why she can’t picture herself doing great things. Her end goal in life is of course to “Be doing something that makes me fulfilled”, but she is so thoughtful in that she wants to work hard these next few years after graduation to make enough money to help her parents retire, especially her Dad, who is a mid-50 year old surgeon constantly working under high stress and for long hours. She wants to end up running her own company that provides some sort of soft-ware as a service, because having experience with clothing and inventory, she knows for sure she doesn’t want to create something that has a physical product. We’ve talked about hopefully in 15-20 years we can start and run our own company together, because we work very well, efficiently, and we know how to communicate with each other and how to get. shit. done.
