Julia Portis, from St Paul, Minnesota

Went to Mounds Park Academy High School

When and how we met: I met Julia Portis Spring semester of sophomore year 2021. I remember having a conversation with her in an uber on the way to Boston about our mental states, our thoughts on frienships at Babson, and what we were seeking in what we believed was a good and fulfilling life in college. We clicked in the car because were both at similar mental points in our lives. I met her at a time in my life where I didn’t have much direction in who I was, who I wanted to be, and my place at Babson. Having this conversation with Julia at this time felt so needed and felt so right, because we both were struggling and we both felt stuck; all we needed was to lean on each other and have each other and we didn’t even know it! But I am grateful to have had her at that time and place, Julia Portis thank you for being my right person at the right time gal.

Why Julia inspires me: Julia’s focus on self-growth, self-confidence, and self-love/sustainability has been something I’ve looked up to since I met her. She lives a life where even though she is focusing on growing herself and pushing the bounds for discovering her potential, she always leaves room for other people and gives care and love to people 110%. She has a beautiful ability to connect with anyone and make everyone feel comfortable, welcomed, and at home. While working on herself and caring for other people, she is also one of the most academically talented people I know. She is incredibly knowledgeable, curious, and an extraordinary writer. I will always look up to the way she holds herself and expresses herself. Julia balances it all, school, friends, work, and play.

Dec 2022 What is Julia up to: In highschool Julia taught at Breakthrough Collaborative Twin Cities which she said was the hardest summer job of her life, but this is where her passion for education sparked. Venturing from Minnesota to the east coast, Julia is now a Presidential Scholar at Babson College and is working on writing her honors thesis—she is researching the impact that test scores (measured over time) and self-efficacy have on academic achievement and sense of self for middle school ELL (english language learning) students. At Babson, she is a member of Kappa Kappa Gamma, works at the writing center, and babysits. After graduating in May 2023, she will move to Boston to work for Accenture as a Consulting Analyst.
